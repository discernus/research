## üìú Framework Overview

The Civic Analysis Framework (CAF) v7.3 is designed to systematically evaluate the civic character of political discourse. Grounded in classical civic republican theory and virtue ethics, it assesses discourse across five bipolar axes: Dignity ‚Üî Tribalism, Truth ‚Üî Manipulation, Justice ‚Üî Resentment, Hope ‚Üî Fear, and Pragmatism ‚Üî Fantasy. Each dimension is scored on a 0.0-1.0 scale, with higher scores indicating the presence of the virtue and lower scores indicating its pathological counterpart. The framework utilizes a sequential chain-of-thought methodology, focusing on linguistic markers to identify semantic patterns. Key derived metrics include tension scores (e.g., Dignity-Tribalism Tension), a Civic Character Index (average of tension scores), a Virtue Index, and a Pathology Index. Pattern classifications (e.g., High Civic Character, Mixed Character) are applied based on these scores to categorize discourse.
