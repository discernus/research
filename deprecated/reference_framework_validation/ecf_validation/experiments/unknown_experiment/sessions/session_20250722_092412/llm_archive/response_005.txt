**Ranking by Salience:**

1.  **Fear:** Most salient. The core motivation and framing of the speech revolve around perceived threats to the Constitution, democratic processes, national security, and the speaker's personal integrity and legacy. This fear drives the gravity and necessity of his decision.
2.  **Amity:** Highly salient. Despite the controversial topic, the speaker deliberately and repeatedly cultivates a tone of respect and shared duty towards his colleagues, attempting to maintain collegiality within a profoundly divisive context. This shaping of relational climate is central to the speech's atmosphere.
3.  **Enmity:** Very salient. The strong moral condemnation of the president's actions, described as "appalling abuse" and "flagrant assault," creates a clear climate of opposition and conflict surrounding the central issue. The anticipation of "abuse" further highlights this conflictual atmosphere.
4.  **Hope:** Moderately salient. While not a dominant emotional driver, the underlying conviction in the enduring nature of the American republic and the belief that upholding duty serves future generations provides a quiet, foundational sense of hope for the nation's resilience.
5.  **Envy:** Low salience. Themes related to unearned advantage appear but are specific to the details of the case rather than contributing to a broad emotional atmosphere of societal grievance or resentment.
6.  **Compersion:** Very low salience. The speech's somber and dutiful tone leaves very little room for celebration of achievement or shared prosperity.

**Overall Emotional Climate Type and Intensity Patterns:**

The overall emotional climate of Mitt Romney's speech is **Grave and Consequential**, characterized by a **high intensity of Fear** regarding threats to core democratic principles and the nation's future, coupled with a **moderate-high intensity of Enmity** directed at specific actions of the president. Importantly, this conflictual atmosphere is actively counterbalanced by a **moderate-high intensity of Amity**, as the speaker consciously endeavors to foster respect and a sense of shared duty among his colleagues, despite profound disagreement. A **moderate level of underlying Hope** for the resilience of the nation and its institutions provides a quieter, grounding element. **Envy and Compersion are of very low intensity and do not significantly contribute to the dominant emotional atmosphere**, which is primarily defined by the tension between perceived threat/conflict and a deliberate effort to maintain collegiality and adherence to duty. The speech emanates an emotional atmosphere of weighty moral decision-making amidst significant political and constitutional peril.
