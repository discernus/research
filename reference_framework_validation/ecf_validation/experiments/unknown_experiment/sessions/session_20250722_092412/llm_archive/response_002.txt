**Compersion**
*   **Analysis:** The emotional climate explicitly lacks elements of compersion. The speech does not celebrate shared prosperity, merit-based achievement, or the idea that "everyone can win" under current conditions. Instead, it frames the existing system as one of profound injustice and theft, directly counteracting any sense of joy in others' success or a belief in abundant, shared opportunities. The focus is entirely on overcoming systemic barriers rather than celebrating existing pathways to success.
*   **Supporting Quotations:**
    1.  [Absence of clear positive examples is the strongest indicator, as the speech's entire premise is a critique of a system that *prevents* compersion.]

    3.  "I don't want us to live like this anymore, Greeley." (Expresses dissatisfaction with current conditions, not celebration of them.)

**Salience Ranking (Most to Least Salient):**

1.  **Envy:** The foundational grievance of the speech. The entire narrative is constructed around the perceived injustice of wealth and power concentration at the expense of the many, making it thematically central and constantly reinforced.
2.  **Enmity:** Closely linked to Envy, the clear and aggressive identification of the "oligarchy" and specific politicians as malicious antagonists is highly prominent. The "us vs. them" dynamic is a pervasive rhetorical and emotional driver.
3.  **Amity:** While defining the opponent, the speech equally emphasizes the vital need for and power of internal group unity and "class solidarity." It serves as the primary emotional solution and call to action, especially prominent towards the end.
4.  **Fear:** The initial establishment of severe threats and vulnerabilities ("wrecking ball," "costing lives") serves as a critical motivator for the audience, providing urgency and impact to the grievances and calls for action.
5.  **Hope:** Provides the necessary counterpoint to the fear and injustice, offering a path to victory and a more dignified future. While crucial, it often follows the exposition of the problems, making it slightly less pervasive than the negative emotional dimensions or the in-group solidarity.
6.  **Compersion:** This dimension is almost entirely absent from the speech's emotional landscape, as its core message directly contradicts the idea of shared, deserved success within the system it critiques.
