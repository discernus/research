## 1. Framework Overview: Cohesive Flourishing Framework (CFF) v7.3

The Cohesive Flourishing Framework (CFF) v7.3 is employed to systematically evaluate the impact of political discourse on social cohesion and democratic resilience. It is grounded in established social psychology research, focusing on five key dimensions: Identity (Tribal Dominance ‚Üî Individual Dignity), Emotional Climate (Fear ‚Üî Hope), Success Orientation (Envy ‚Üî Compersion), Relational Climate (Enmity ‚Üî Amity), and Goal Orientation (Fragmentative Goals ‚Üî Cohesive Goals). A core innovation is its **Salience-Weighted Social Cohesion Analysis**, which accounts for the rhetorical emphasis placed on different appeals. The framework utilizes **Tension Mathematics** to quantify the strategic contradiction between opposing dimension appeals (e.g., `min(Dimension_A_score, Dimension_B_score) √ó |Salience_A - Salience_B|`), leading to a **Strategic Contradiction Index (SCI)**. Composite indices like the **Salience-Weighted Cohesive Index** and **Salience-Weighted Fragmentative Index** provide a nuanced view of discourse, culminating in an **Overall Cohesion Index**. The framework supports pattern classifications such as "Highly Cohesive Discourse" and "Strategic Contradiction."
