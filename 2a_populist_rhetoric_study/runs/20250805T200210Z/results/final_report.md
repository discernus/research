## üî¨ Framework Overview: Populist Discourse Analysis Framework (PDAF) v7.3

The Populist Discourse Analysis Framework (PDAF) v7.3 is designed to systematically quantify populist rhetorical patterns across ideological divides. It moves beyond simplistic binary classifications by focusing on nine core dimensions organized into Primary Populist Core Anchors, Populist Mechanism Anchors, and Boundary Distinction Anchors. A key innovation is its **populist strategic tension analysis**, which measures how speakers deploy populist appeals, including potential contradictions. The framework employs an extended 0.0-2.0 scale for intensity and uses **dynamic salience weighting**. Calculated metrics include various composite indices (Core Populism, Populist Mechanisms, Boundary Distinctions) and strategic tension scores. The framework's analytical approach involves sequential chain-of-thought, focusing on each dimension group before integration.

## üìä Executive Summary

This comprehensive analysis of Donald J. Trump's presidential speeches from 2017 to 2025, utilizing the Populist Discourse Analysis Framework (PDAF) v7.3, reveals a consistent and strong engagement with populist themes across all analyzed documents. The **Overall Populism Index** averaged a high 0.80, indicating a significant presence of populist appeals. Notably, **Manichaean People-Elite Framing** emerged as a dominant dimension (mean score 0.88), consistently portraying a dichotomy between a virtuous "people" and corrupt "elites" [1, 2, 3]. This framing strongly correlates with **Nationalist Exclusion** (r=0.97) and **Popular Sovereignty Claims** (r=0.97), suggesting a strategy of defining an in-group against perceived external or internal threats and bypassing established political structures.

Significant differences in populist discourse were observed across speech contexts (p=0.032), with Inaugural and Joint Session addresses showing higher overall populism scores compared to State of the Union addresses. This aligns with the experiment's hypothesis that context matters. However, the analysis was hampered by the inability to calculate the **Populist Strategic Contradiction Index (PSCI)** due to missing data, preventing a full assessment of strategic populist contradictions. Despite this limitation, the findings underscore a persistent, ideologically focused populist communication strategy.
